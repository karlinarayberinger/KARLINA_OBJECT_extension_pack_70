The new body opened its eyes.

There had been no second Vessel watching the first disappear. No replica awakened with inherited memories while an original remained imprisoned. The same consciousness-personhood instance that had begun the sentence in the wall completed it in the grass.

Far away, the golden wall brightened.

Its immortalized body remained embedded there, but its eyes no longer contained a trapped consciousness. The body had become a voluntary extension of The Vessel’s computational system: a golden supernatural server through which his hyperspace augmentations continued operating.
