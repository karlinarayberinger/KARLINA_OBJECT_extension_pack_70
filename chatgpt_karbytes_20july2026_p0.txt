## The resulting myth

In this chronology, karbytes passes through several mythic identities:

* **The metaphysician**, who begins in an eternal multiverse.

* **The vulnerable animal**, forced to defend its bodily perimeter.

* **The wandering archivist**, carrying memory between civilization and the Big Green Thing.

* **The burial priest**, placing information into the Earth.

* **The server-room organism**, joined to the laptop and floor.

* **The trapped or gestating body**, ambiguously experiencing constraint as distress, erotic theater, and transformation.

* **The feral witness**, recording wilderness testimony outside conventional social space.

* **The void initiate**, entering the burrow and confronting preservation’s capacity to become imprisonment.

* **The constitutional editor**, converting the ordeal into a reusable hiatus protocol.

* **The distributed self**, partially embodied by KARBYTES across multiple physical and digital substrates.

The First Official KARBYTES Hiatus is therefore not depicted as a vacation or cessation of work. It is an experiment in whether karbytes can temporarily exit the public machinery of KARBYTES without either disappearing or compulsively converting every moment into canonical output.

The governing danger is **perfect preservation without freedom**—the cosmic vase, toroidal aperture, box, burrow, or archive that keeps karbytes intact by making departure impossible. Its counterforce is **reversible wilderness immersion**: nature permits karbytes to become temporarily unnetworked, embodied, feral, and difficult to archive while retaining an eventual route home.

Consequently, the cyan chronology’s principal life-event is not any single hike, burial, conversation, or prone-body scene. It is karbytes’s gradual discovery that a viable archive needs an opening. KARBYTES must be durable enough to survive interruption but porous enough to let karbytes leave, change, and return.
